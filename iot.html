Internet of Things (IoT)

When everyday objects are connected to the internet

What is the Internet of Things?

The Internet of Things or IoT means connecting everyday objects to the internet so they can send and receive data. In 2026, your fridge, lights, door locks, TV, air conditioner, and even your shoes can be connected to the internet!

When devices are connected, they can communicate with each other and with you through your phone. This makes life more convenient and can save energy.

IoT Devices in Your Home

Smart Fridge

A smart fridge can see what food is inside it. It can tell you when food is about to expire and even automatically order new food from an online shop when you are running out!

Smart Lights

Smart lights can be controlled with your phone from anywhere in the world. They can also turn on and off automatically based on whether anyone is home or what time of day it is.

Smart Door Locks

Smart locks can open your door automatically when you arrive home. You can also unlock your door for a visitor using your phone even when you are not home.

Smart Cities - IoT on a Big Scale

A Smart City uses IoT technology throughout the entire city to make it work better. In 2026, many cities are becoming smart cities.

Smart City Technology What it Does
Smart Traffic Lights Change timing based on how many cars are waiting to reduce traffic jams
Smart Street Lights Only turn on when someone is nearby to save electricity
Smart Waste Bins Send a signal when they are full so garbage trucks know when to come
Smart Water Pipes Detect water leaks automatically to prevent waste
Air Quality Sensors Monitor pollution levels throughout the city in real time

How Many IoT Devices Exist?

In 2026, there are estimated to be over 30 billion IoT devices connected to the internet around the world! That is more than 3 devices for every single person on Earth.
